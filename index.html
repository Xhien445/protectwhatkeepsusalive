“If the ocean dies, we die.”

SDG 14, Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development, asks us to protect the ocean from pollution, overfishing and climate change. The quote by marine conservation activist Captain Paul Watson, “If the ocean dies, we die,” is not just a metaphor: the ocean produces much of the oxygen we breathe, regulates the climate, and feeds millions of people.

Protect
What Keeps
Us Alive

Yet our own actions — plastic and chemical waste, destructive fishing, coastal development, and burning fossil fuels that warm and acidify the sea — are killing marine ecosystems and the animals that hold them together. When coral reefs bleach, when sharks are finned, when fish stocks collapse, it is not only “life below water” that disappears; it is our food, our safety, and our future that sink with it.
